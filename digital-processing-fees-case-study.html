Conclusion

Digital convenience is now an expectation, not an option. Small businesses cannot refuse cards without losing customers, yet accepting them erodes profits. George's work with NeoPay shows how one person can push back, but it also reveals how deeply embedded the fee structure is. Understanding that tension helps us see the hidden pressures behind every transaction.

Reflection

Working on this project helped me understand how complex digital payment systems are and how difficult they can be for small businesses to navigate. Meeting George Peacock and learning about his journey to build NeoPay gave me a concrete example of how processing fees create real financial pressure. Translating that into a clear narrative meant simplifying a deliberately complicated system while balancing text, video, and design so each element added something new.

The process showed how digital convenience shapes everyday business operations and why small financial pressures can add up to a larger structural problem. Grounding the package in George's experience made that dynamic tangible for audiences who usually only see the customer side of the checkout counter.
