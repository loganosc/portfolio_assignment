Digital Processing Fees & The Small Business Squeeze

A final project exploring how rising card-processing fees erode small business margins and what clear communication, policy literacy, and visual storytelling can do to keep owners in control.

  • Role: Researcher, Writer, & Designer
  • Format: Full WordPress digital package
  • Timeline: 4 weeks
  • Tools: WordPress, Adobe Illustrator, Premiere Pro
Back to portfolio Skip to sources

Introduction

Credit-card and digital wallet fees seem invisible until they stack up at the end of the month. For this package I set out to make the hidden costs tangible, walking readers through real stories of small retailers, bakeries, and service providers who are navigating higher interchange rates while trying to stay transparent with their communities.

Video Overview

I led with a short video explainer to ground the topic before diving into the data, pairing narration with footage from local shops and animated overlays that unpack how processing fees accumulate.

A two-minute walkthrough of how a few cents per transaction scales into thousands over a year.

Article Body

The written package is structured in concise, scannable blocks. Each paragraph pairs a grounded story with a data point so the narrative stays empathetic without losing the economic stakes.

Paragraph two highlights how restaurants facing thin margins have to decide between absorbing fees or raising menu prices. Owner interviews revealed that customers often confuse processing surcharges with price gouging.

$7,200 Average annual processing fees for a neighborhood café running $30 tickets.

Paragraph Three

I break down state-by-state rules on surcharging and cash-discount programs, linking to a compliance checklist so owners can avoid fines while staying transparent with customers.

Paragraph Four

The final block explores design interventions—like receipt annotations and in-store signage—that keep customers informed and reduce frustration at checkout.

Infographic

A full-width infographic visualizes how a 2.9% + $0.30 fee structure impacts businesses at different average ticket sizes. It also contrasts processor markups to demystify “discounted rates.”

Reflection & Closing

The package closes with a short reflection that frames processing fees as a systemic challenge rather than a merchant failure. By pairing reporting with approachable visuals, the goal is to give owners language to advocate for themselves—whether negotiating with processors or educating their neighborhoods.
